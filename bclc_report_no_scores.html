What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether the work you actually do is set up to benefit from AI – so it can save you time, raise the quality of what your team produces, and give you back the hours that should be going to leadership, fundraising, and the people work only you can do.

For that to happen, five things need to be in place:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't help if the information you need lives in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your families and community. Who they are, what they need, and how they find you. AI can't help you serve people you don't understand.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried a few things, you're curious, and you want to move. This is the input that makes the rest of the system actually get used.

Where the leverage is

The opportunity areas

Four places where the diagnostic surfaced real leverage, sized to what an Executive Director of a licensed non-profit early education center can realistically deploy. These are observations and directions, not a finished spec – the AI Plan is where they get prioritized, sequenced, and turned into something concrete.

1

Direct Reports & Project Tracking System

Anchor opportunity · Speaks to the magic wand answer

The most direct answer to your magic wand. A lightweight weekly check-in cadence with each of your direct reports – Director of Education, Lead Educators, Administration – with AI-drafted prep notes pulled from the previous week's notes, a status rollup you read in 5 minutes instead of 50, and a shared tracker for the 8–12 active initiatives BCLC is running at any time (grant deliverables, capital projects, program enhancements, hiring searches, Cummings Foundation reporting milestones, QRIS work). The opportunity is to take the project and people visibility you named and build it as a real system – running in tools BCLC already pays for (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365), not a new platform. "Easily manageable" is the design rule. The exact shape (which AI tool, which initiatives first, what the cadence looks like) is what the AI Plan would work out.

2

ED Admin & Compliance Drafting Layer

Time-leverage opportunity · Speaks to the operational headache

Your stated operational headache was staying on top of paperwork and admin – invoicing, compliance, documentation, follow-ups. For an ED, that maps to a specific recurring set: monthly board reports, Cummings Foundation grant reporting (live and recurring as a 2025 multi-year grantee), QRIS quality submissions, parent and family communication memos, staff communications, EEC compliance documentation drafts, fundraising copy. The opportunity is to compress that surface – AI-drafted first passes, you reviewing and editing, your voice and judgment intact. Inputs are your own notes, prior-version documents, and BCLC's public information; never family or child data, never staff PII, never anything that would touch confidentiality. The Plan would scope which writing surfaces matter most and how aggressively to compress them.

3

Institutional Memory & Knowledge Capture

Risk-reduction opportunity · Speaks to the 4-out-of-5 key-person risk

The 4-out-of-5 key-person-risk answer is the most consequential signal in the diagnostic. Twenty-six years of institutional memory at BCLC – financial history, donor relationships, building and facilities knowledge, board dynamics, EEC and QRIS history, vendor relationships, parent and community context – currently lives in your head. The opportunity is a structured capture of that knowledge into a navigable internal resource – AI-assisted templates for documenting recurring decisions and the reasoning behind them, an annotated archive for the major moves (the building purchase, the Cummings grant, major program changes), a transition-resilience playbook tied to specific roles. This isn't "replace what's in your head" – it's the responsible move of a CPA-trained ED who plans for continuity. If you're on vacation, on sabbatical, or eventually transitioning, BCLC doesn't lose the why behind the how.

4

Family Engagement & Community Visibility System

Growth opportunity · The Chamber + Cummings footprint

The diagnostic flagged two real gaps in how families and community visibility are tracked: review monitoring is "manual when someone remembers," and referrals are "informal" rather than structured. The opportunity is one system that fits how an early ed non-profit actually grows – family referrals, Google reviews, parent testimonials, partner-program visibility (Beverly Public Schools, Endicott College, North Shore United Way), Greater Beverly Chamber engagement, Cummings Foundation grantee profile activation. AI drafts the asks, the templates, and the communications; you approve before anything goes out. Includes a structured 90-day review-monitoring cadence, a quarterly testimonial-capture flow tied to family enrollment milestones, and a Chamber engagement rhythm – the same Chamber Crane partnered with for the April pop-up at 100 Cummings Center, ~0.6 miles from your facility at 550 Cabot Street. Uses zero family or child data; everything operates at the public-and-aggregate level.
