What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether the work you actually do is set up to benefit from AI – so it saves you time, raises the quality your team produces, and gives you back the hours for the leadership and people work only you can do.

Five conditions matter:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't work with information that lives only in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your families and community. Who they are, what they need, and how they find you.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried things, you're curious, and you want to move.

ED Admin, State Contract & Reporting Drafting Layer

Time-leverage opportunity · Speaks to the operational headache

3

Institutional Memory & Succession Knowledge Capture

Most time-sensitive opportunity · Speaks to the key-person risk and retirement horizon
AI Readiness Check Strategy session

Thirty years of institutional knowledge at BCLC – financial history, donor and grant relationships, board dynamics, the family daycare network, audit history – currently lives in your head. The diagnostic flagged this as 4-out-of-5 key-person risk; you named retirement on the horizon during the strategy session. Together that makes this the most time-sensitive opportunity in the report. Not "replace what's in your head" – make sure BCLC doesn't lose the why behind the how. The work spans two layers: structured capture of decisions as you make them going forward, and a historical archive of the major moves already behind you (the Cummings grant, the family daycare network, audit and funding history) – tied together by a transition-resilience playbook organized by role.

4

Program Evaluation & Reporting Aggregation

Direct-from-the-strategy-session opportunity
Strategy session

A use case you raised in your own words: required evaluations on children, environment, teachers, and classrooms come back as anecdotes ("5 instances where children progressed by 1%") when you've been asking for aggregate rollups at the classroom and program level. A workflow that turns the evaluation data already being collected into the program-, classroom-, teacher-level, and environmental rollups you need – on the cadence the state requires plus any internal cadence that's useful. Inputs are rubrics, observation notes, and structured fields from the childcare management system; outputs are rollup views and summary narratives. Operates on aggregate and de-identified data only – never individual child records.
